University Secures
£2M Grant for Renewable
Energy Research

Apply Now
Enquire Today

Published: 10 August 2025

By: Dr. Sarah Williams

Time: 4 min read

Our Faculty of Engineering has been awarded a £2 million grant to advance renewable energy research. This project will develop sustainable solutions for urban energy systems, impacting communities across the UK.

About the Competition

The National Innovation Award, hosted annually by the British Council for Business, attracts over 200 entries from universities across the UK.
The competition challenges students to develop market-ready solutions to global problems, with a focus on sustainability, technology, and community impact.
This year’s theme — “Innovating for a Greener Future” — saw record participation, reflecting the growing urgency of environmental action in the business sector.

The Winning Idea: GreenLedger

“GreenLedger” uses blockchain technology to provide real-time verification of supply chain sustainability, giving companies and consumers confidence in the environmental impact of the products they purchase.
The system also integrates with existing enterprise software, making adoption easy for businesses of all sizes.

“We wanted to create something practical, scalable, and transparent,” said team leader Amira Khan, a final-year BSc Business Management student. “GreenLedger gives power back to the consumer while helping businesses meet their sustainability goals.”

The Team Behind the Project

The winning team — Amira Khan, Lucas Martin, and Priya Desai — combined their skills in business analytics, entrepreneurship, and marketing to develop the project.

“It was truly a cross-disciplinary effort,” said Lucas. “Our mentors from the Business School and the Computer Science Department played a huge role in helping us refine the concept.”

Support from the University

  • Access to workspace and research resources
  • Weekly mentorship sessions
  • Funding for prototype development
  • Opportunities to present to industry experts

Dr. Sarah Williams, Director of the Innovation & Enterprise Hub, praised the team's dedication:

"They have shown that with creativity, determination, and collaboration, our students can compete at the very highest level nationally."

What’s Next for GreenLedger
Following their win, the team will receive:

  • A £10,000 seed grant to further develop the platform
  • One-on-one mentorship from industry leaders in fintech and sustainability
  • An invitation to present at the Global Student Innovation Forum in Berlin this October

The students are also in early talks with two UK-based retailers to pilot GreenLedger in real-world supply chains.
